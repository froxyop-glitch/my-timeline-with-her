A note, mostly for you

I don't remember most Tuesdays. I don't remember what we ate two weeks ago, or what the weather was doing on any given afternoon. But I remember the shape of the years — the apartment that was too small, the trip we couldn't afford, the mountain we probably shouldn't have climbed.

Mostly I remember choosing this, again, on the ordinary days when no one was watching. That's the part I'd do over, exactly the same, every time.

— S.
